Gender and the torch relay

No woman has ever carried the Olympic flame down from Olympia as the first torchbearer in a torch relay. However, out of the more than 50 final torchbearers that have lit the cauldron during an opening ceremony in 80 years, 14 were women. Norma Enriqueta Basiio became the first woman to light an Olympic cauldron in 1968 during the Mexico City Games. Each outline of a runner represents a single torchbearer and relay, except in 2002 where an indeterminate number of members from the 1980 ‘Miracle on Ice’ hockey team lit the cauldron. A few relays featured multiple final torchbearers.
